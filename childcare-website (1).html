Our Approach

The Early Years Learning Framework

All our programs are built on the Australian Government's Early Years Learning Framework (EYLF) — "Belonging, Being and Becoming." This national framework ensures every child at Childcare receives intentional, high-quality early education.

B

Belonging

Every child and family feels connected, valued and welcomed — especially important in our rich multicultural community.

B

Being

We celebrate and honour each child's current stage of childhood, not just who they are becoming.

B

Becoming

We nurture growth and identity — supporting children to develop skills, knowledge and confidence at their own pace.

🥗

Quality Kitchen — Every Day

Our full-service quality kitchen prepares fresh, nutritious meals and snacks every day. We cater to dietary requirements and ensure all children enjoy wholesome food that respects their family's values.

Safety First, Always

Our centre meets and exceeds all NSW regulatory requirements. All educators hold valid Working With Children Checks, and our premises are regularly safety-audited for your child's protection.

Our Origin

Why We Started Childcare

Childcare was founded with a clear mission: to provide Strathfield families with high-quality, inclusive early education in a warm and welcoming environment. From the very beginning, we set out to be a centre that genuinely reflected our community.

We recognised that many families in Strathfield come from diverse cultural backgrounds — and that finding a centre with a quality kitchen, culturally sensitive care and qualified educators who understood their needs was both difficult and deeply important.

Over more than two decades, we've built something special: a centre where children thrive, families trust us deeply, and educators love coming to work. We are proud of our long-standing reputation and even prouder of the many children who started their journey with us.

Family Stories

A Snapshot From One of Our Families

🎒 From Shy Toddler to Confident Kindergartener

When Mariam's parents first enrolled her at Childcare at age 2, she was extremely shy and had difficulty separating from her mother. The nursery and early preschool teams worked patiently over many months to build her trust and confidence.

By the time she entered our School Readiness program at age 4, Mariam was one of the most enthusiastic participants in every group activity. She started Kindergarten at a local Strathfield primary school last year — her teacher told her family she was one of the most prepared and socially capable students in the class.

"We never imagined she'd come this far. Childcare changed everything." — Mariam's Mum

🥗 Finding a Centre That Felt Like Home

For Ahmed and his family — practising Muslims from Strathfield — finding a childcare centre with a quality kitchen and a culturally respectful environment was non-negotiable.

After visiting several centres in the area, they found Childcare. The certified quality kitchen, the warm welcome from staff, and the centre's long track record in the local community gave them the confidence they needed.

Three years on, both their children have thrived here. Ahmed describes Childcare as "an extension of our family — a place where our values are not just tolerated but celebrated."

"It's more than childcare. It's community." — Ahmed, Strathfield Parent
